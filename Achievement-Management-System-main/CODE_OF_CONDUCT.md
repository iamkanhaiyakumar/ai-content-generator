# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

We strive to:
- Be respectful and inclusive
- Focus on constructive feedback
- Help newcomers learn
- Maintain professional communication

## Expected Behavior

### Welcoming and Inclusive
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Respect differences in opinion and perspective
- Be mindful of different cultural experiences and communication styles

### Constructive Feedback
- Provide feedback in a constructive and respectful manner
- Focus on what is best for the community and project
- Acknowledge that different approaches may be valid for different contexts

### Helping Others Learn
- Be patient with newcomers and those asking questions
- Provide explanations and guidance when needed
- Remember that everyone was once a beginner

### Professional Communication
- Keep discussions focused on technical matters
- Avoid personal attacks or derogatory language
- Assume good intentions from others

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in our community:

- Harassment or discrimination
- Spam or off-topic content
- Sharing inappropriate content
- Trolling or disruptive behavior

## Enforcement

Actions that violate these standards may result in:
- Private warning from maintainers
- Temporary or permanent suspension of access
- Removal of offending content
- In severe cases, expulsion from the community

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated fairly.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please contact the maintainers directly through GitHub issues or private communication channels.

All reports will be handled with discretion and sensitivity to the privacy of those involved.
